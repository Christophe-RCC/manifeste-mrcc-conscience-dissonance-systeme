# Manifesto of the Complexed Causal Reaction (MRCC)

> **A Unifying Model for Systemic Regulation of Humanity**  
> *"How does causality explain phenomena in a deterministic and probabilistic universe?*  
> *If knowledge, emotions, and thoughts are recognized as physical phenomena, then causality becomes the sole mechanism capable of explaining our behaviors and the genesis of our current problems."*  

**Consciousness, Dissonance, and Systemic Regulation**  
The MRCC postulates that consciousness is a mechanism for detecting energetic dissonance. From the synapse to society, the illusion of free will wastes energy. This manifesto proposes a causal approach to optimize our structures and avoid collapse.

**Dissonance as Thermal Agitation**

Dissonance is not simply "sadness." It is a physical state of high agitation. When a system's internal model (its beliefs) conflicts with reality, the matter composing it enters turbulence.

Imagine a fluid trying to flow uphill: molecules collide, vibrate, heat up, and consume colossal energy to maintain an impossible position. This is the state of suffering: a struggle against the laws of causality, like fighting gravity without understanding it.

**Understanding as Fluidification**

Accepting causality means ceasing to fight the current. The system aligns with reality. Internal "vibrations" (stress, conflict, guilt) subside. The energy wasted on internal friction is now freed for movement, creation, and regulation.

The system does not become "dead" or static; it becomes fluid. It dissipates entropy outwardly in an efficient manner, rather than trapping it internally as destructive heat (since heat is what every system tends to reduce to increase stability. It generates more entropy in return, which is consistent with thermodynamics).

Healing is therefore not the stopping of movement, but the end of turbulence.

## Introduction: The Universal Law of Energy

The MRCC postulates that the same physical law of **minimizing prediction error** governs all complex systems, regardless of their scale.

Every complex and open system (from the atom to the galaxy, passing through a living organism or a society) does not tend toward a static equilibrium (which would be its death), but toward a **dynamic stationary state of optimal dissipation**.

Every system tends toward the state that minimizes friction between its internal model and observed reality (the context).  
The interpretation of this model provides a coherent, unifying explanation for black holes, societies, AI, and photon behavior.  
The visible effect differs depending on the context (scale), but the logic is always the same.

These systems seek to:
1.  Minimize local energy gradients.
2.  Maximize the efficiency of their flow to maintain their structure far from thermodynamic equilibrium.

> **Nature does not optimize for rest, but for the resilience of the flow.**  
> It organizes energy flows to reduce internal friction (dissonance) and maintain structural stability against external perturbations.

Humanity, as a complex system of living matter and information, is no exception to this law. However, we observe a paradox: the more we try to solve our crises (climate, social, psychological), the more global tension seems to increase. We fight symptoms, spending colossal energy to maintain unstable structures.

This manifesto proposes changing our interpretation model. By shifting from a modeling based on the illusion of free will to one based on **complex causality**, the system ceases to generate unnecessary dissonance (judgment, guilt, conflict) and begins to use its energy to address real causes.

## Chapter 1: Consciousness as an Emergent Phenomenon of Dissonance (The Feeling)

If consciousness is not a supernatural entity, but an emergent phenomenon of complex matter, it must obey the laws of thermodynamics. It is not an "actor" acting upon reality, but the physical feeling of the system's energy state.  
In this model, consciousness is not an "entity" observing the body from the outside; it is the body itself feeling its own regulation state. There is no separation between the one who feels and what is felt: it is a single physical process, observed from the inside.  

**Consciousness is the subjective translation of systemic dissonance.**

Every complex system (brain, society, algorithm) inevitably tends toward its state of least energy (principle of least action). Consciousness emerges when this system possesses sufficient complexity to feel the gap between its current state and its equilibrium state.

In this light, thought is not a mysterious activity of a "soul," but a sense in its own right, just like touch, sight, or smell.

*   **Touch** detects mechanical constraints on the skin.
*   **Sight** detects photons on the retina.
*   **Thought** detects logical incoherencies and model gaps in the information flow.

Just as skin pain signals a burn (thermal dissonance), mental pain signals cognitive dissonance. Just as sight allows navigation in physical space, thought allows navigation in the space of causes and effects.

### The Signals of Thought

*   **Pain (Systemic Tension):** This is the physical sensation of unresolved dissonance. It is the signal that the system is expending energy to maintain an unstable state. Whether it is an overactive neuron, an overheating thermostat, or a human in internal conflict: it is the same physical reality. Pain is the feeling of local entropy (This does not mean a thermostat "feels" pain in the human sense. It detects a physical tension (a thermal gradient). This detection is a primitive form of systemic feeling: the capacity of a system to signal a gap between its internal state and external reality, without possessing complex emotional consciousness).  
*   **Confusion:** This is the sensation of configuration search. The system explores states to reduce tension. It is the feeling of uncertainty before stabilization, similar to an eye trying to focus.
*   **Joy (Relaxation):** This is the sensation of dissonance reduction. It is the signal that the system has found a more stable, energy-efficient configuration. The return to the state of least energy, comparable to the relief of a muscle tension.

*   **Critical Note:** Suffering/Pain is not a guarantee of truth.   
  It is crucial to note that pain, while a driver of change, does not automatically guide the system toward objective truth. It guides the system toward the reduction of dissonance.

If an illusion (such as a dogmatic belief, a myth of free will, or a punitive religion) allows for the reduction of pain more efficiently and quickly than raw reality, the system will choose that illusion. This explains why complex systems (like entire societies) can remain stuck in erroneous beliefs for centuries: the illusion offers a locally stable equilibrium state that avoids the immediate dissonance of truth.

Truth only emerges when the cost of maintaining the illusion (the dissonance accumulated by denying reality) becomes greater than the cost of accepting the truth. The MRCC Model does not postulate that suffering leads to wisdom, but that it forces the system to seek a solution, whether true or false, depending on what is most "energy-efficient" at that moment.


**Fundamental Consequence:** Consciousness does not "seek" truth for pleasure. It is the thermometer of internal coherence.

*   If observed reality conflicts with the internal model → **Dissonance** → Pain.
*   If reality aligns with the internal model → **Equilibrium** → Joy/Cal.

Thus, consciousness no longer needs to be immaterial. It is purely physical. It is the way a complex system feels its own thermodynamic regulation. Thought is simply the organ of detection of this regulation, an internal sense that allows the system to adjust without needing an external "conductor." What we call "will" is merely the information flow necessary to minimize this dissonance, perceived by this internal sense.

### Physical Precision Note: Informational Dissonance vs. Thermodynamic Entropy

It is crucial to distinguish the dissonance defined here from classical thermodynamic entropy.

*   **Entropy** is a measurable physical quantity (in Joules/Kelvin) that quantifies molecular disorder or energy unavailability in a closed system.
*   **Dissonance**, within the MRCC framework, is an informational and systemic measure. It represents the gap between the system's internal model (its beliefs, laws, neural connections) and observed reality.

Although reducing this dissonance often tends to minimize global entropy (by optimizing energy flows), the two concepts are not identical. A system can be locally ordered (low entropy) while being in high dissonance (its internal model is false relative to reality). The MRCC postulates that consciousness is the signal of this informational gap. This distinction allows the model to be applied to cognitive and social systems without violating thermodynamic laws, while explaining why a system can "overheat" (spend energy) even in a physically stable environment.

## Chapter 2: The Fractal Scale of Reality

The structure of reality is fractal. The same physical laws governing the microscopic govern the macroscopic.

*   **At the Micro Level (The Neuron):** A neuron reacts when it detects a signal incoherent with its state. It expends energy to modify its connections (learning) and reduce tension. This is the basis of memory and thought: a chemical reaction to dissonance, not a decision.
*   **At the Meso Level (The Individual):** A human reacts physically when perceiving (via the "sense of thought") a conflict between their reality and their beliefs. They feel dissonance (stress, anger) and tend to act to reduce it (behavior change, search for meaning). This action is not a "free choice," but the inevitable consequence of the pain signal forcing the system to seek a state of least energy.
*   **At the Macro Level (Society):** Society is a network of interconnected individuals, acting as the "neurons" of a global brain. If social structures (laws, economy, norms) are incoherent with reality or individual needs, society generates systemic dissonance (crises, revolts, instability). Society reacts (reforms, wars, regime changes) to reduce this tension, exactly as an organism reacts to an infection.

**Major Implication:** Society is not a moral abstraction. It is a physical phenomenon. One cannot "govern" it with abstract ideas, but only by adjusting physical parameters (resources, information flows, structures) to reduce dissonance. What is true for the brain is true for civilization. Understanding this physical unity is the key to moving from abstract, inefficient management of our societies to concrete, sustainable regulation.

### Learning Mechanism

*"If consciousness is just a feedback signal, how does the system evolve?"*

Learning is not an act of will, but a structural physical modification triggered by dissonance.

When a system (neuron, individual, society) detects persistent dissonance (pain, crisis), it is physically forced to modify its internal parameters to restore equilibrium:

*   **At the micro level:** It is synaptic plasticity: weak connections weaken, strong ones strengthen, updating the "neuronal model."
*   **At the meso level:** It is behavioral adaptation: the individual modifies habits to avoid future pain.
*   **At the macro level:** It is institutional reform: society changes laws to reduce social tension.

This process is iterative and deterministic. Each reduction of dissonance leaves a physical trace (memory, law, habit) that modifies the system's future response. Evolution is therefore not a "conscious discovery," but the inevitable consequence of regulation by trial-and-error guided by the pain signal. The system "learns" because its physical structure has been forced to adjust to reality to survive.

## Chapter 3: The Illusion of Free Will as a Source of Energy Inefficiency

The fundamental problem of current humanity lies in a false belief: **the illusion of free will.**

We believe our actions are the result of a conscious choice, detached from our prior causes. We think we are the "authors" of our acts, independent of our history, biology, environment, and quantum chance.

This belief creates a **major systemic dissonance**:

*   **Guilt and Judgment:** When we or others make a mistake, we attribute it to a "defect of will" or a "bad decision." We punish, we judge, we guilt-trip. These actions do not correct the cause of the error (the context, conditions, past patterns); they add a layer of additional dissonance (stress, fear, conflict).
*   **Inefficiency of Solutions:** By treating symptoms (punishment, repression, guilt) instead of causes (environmental conditions, traumas, social structures), we increase the system's complexity. We spend enormous energy maintaining an illusion of control, which generates more chaos. The system, believing in free will, acts like an engine idling: it consumes a lot of energy to produce little result, and overheats.

Physical reality is different: Every event is the inevitable result of prior causes (determinism), but quantum indeterminacy and complexity make the future unpredictable (stochastic). **We are not "free" to choose our causes, but we are the inevitable artisans of our effects.**

Understanding this does not create a new "will." On the contrary, it unlocks colossal energy. Instead of wasting energy judging and punishing (which increases dissonance), the system naturally directs this energy toward modifying causes. If we understand that a behavior is the result of a context, we modify the context to change the behavior. This is the path of least energy, a physical reaction to understanding causality.

### Principle of Complete Causality and the Nature of Unpredictability

#### 1. Fundamental Distinction: Epistemic Ignorance vs. Ontological Indeterminism
It is imperative to distinguish the observer's knowledge limitation from the absence of cause in reality.

*   **MRCC Postulate:** Every event (physical, biological, psychic) is the inevitable consequence of a prior causal chain.
*   **Thesis:** The unpredictability of an event does not imply its indeterminism. The perceived "chance" is a measure of the system's **computational complexity** and the **observer's limitation**, not an intrinsic property of reality.
*   **Formulation:** The future is **unpredictable** for a finite system (due to chaos and quantum complexity), but **determined** by the total state of the universe at time $t-1$.

> **Causality Axiom:** "It is not because a cause is unknown or unobservable that it does not exist. The absence of detection is not an absence of causality."

#### 2. Information as a Physical Causal Variable
Within the MRCC framework, information (knowledge, belief, memory) is not an abstract entity outside physical laws.

*   **Principle of Physical Inscription:** All information is physically encoded in matter (e.g., synaptic configuration, quantum state, molecular structure).
*   **Causal Integration:** The fact of "knowing" or "believing" modifies the energy and structural state of the system. This modification becomes a **causal variable** in its own right within the feedback loop.
*   **Consequence:** The observer is not an external spectator possessing free will, but a physical component whose state (including their "knowledge") inevitably determines their interaction with the observed system.

#### 3. Synthesis: Probabilistic Determinism
The MRCC rejects Laplacian determinism (total predictability) while rejecting radical indeterminism (absence of cause).

| Concept | MRCC Definition | Implication |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Determinism** | Every effect has a necessary cause. | The future is not "written" statically, but emerges necessarily from present causes. |
| **Probabilism** | Complexity makes prediction impossible for a finite observer. | Unpredictability is a property of observation, not reality. |
| **Free Will** | Emergent illusion of detection complexity. | Consciousness detects dissonance, but does not "choose" causes. |

**Conclusion:**   
Accepting this principle allows the cognitive dissonance linked to the search for "magical choices" or "absolute chances" to dissolve. Reality is a continuous causal flow, where information is a physical force shaping the future without breaking the law of causality.

**Note on the "Creator" and Causality:**   
The MRCC rejects the idea of an anthropomorphic "God" intervening arbitrarily in causality (miracles, punishments, divine choices), as this would violate the laws of thermodynamics and energy conservation.

However, the model is perfectly compatible with a definition of God as the set of necessary causal laws governing the universe (a view close to Spinoza or Einstein). In this light:   

God is not an "actor" who chooses, but the First Cause from which all other causes inevitably follow.   
Providence is not a magical intervention, but the logical necessity of the causal chain.   
"Design" is not a plan written in advance, but the inevitable emergence of complexity from these laws.   
Thus, accepting probabilistic determinism is not an atheization of reality, but a recognition that the Law itself is the only absolute entity. Suffering is not a divine punishment, but a physical consequence of complexity seeking its equilibrium.

## Mechanisms of Transition: From Resistance to Stabilization

Adopting this model does not happen instantly. It follows a predictable thermodynamic dynamic, marked by phases of resistance and reorganization.

### The Dissonance of the Old Model

The first reactions (judgment, rejection, disbelief, anger) are **logical and inevitable**. They correspond to the resistance of a system whose internal model (free will) is violently perturbed.

*   **The Mechanism:** The brain detects an incoherence between its wired structure (free will) and the new information (causality). This dissonance generates pain (stress, anxiety).
*   **Survival Strategy:** The system tries to maintain its current state (homeostasis) by rejecting the information. This is why systems already saturated (stress, anxiety, trauma) may either collapse or accept the new perspective more easily because the cost of maintaining the old model becomes too high.

**Saturation Risk:** If the idea is rejected but not resolved, it does not disappear. It becomes a **latent cognitive load**.

### The Initial Reaction

*   **The Danger:** An "isolated" idea in the system continues to generate friction. The brain expends energy to keep it at bay, creating chronic dissonance. This can lead to increased defense reactions (anger, flight, anxiety) or system saturation (crisis).
*   **"Attractor Bypass" or "Barrier Jump":** However, the brain's complexity offers an exit. Even an initial rejection does not erase the physical imprint of the information. If, at some point, the system finds a configuration where accepting the MRCC model reduces pain (dissonance reduction), a "breach" opens. It is the attractor bypass: a single successful neural connection can initiate a butterfly effect. Other neurons, seeking to minimize energy, will mimic this more efficient path. "Madness" is not the risk, but the **resistance to adaptation** that leads to collapse.

### The "Yoyo" Phase and the Necessity of Anchoring

For systems beginning the transition, an oscillation phase is inevitable.

*   **The Phenomenon:** The system swings between the old attractor (doubt, return to guilt schemas) and the new one (understanding, calm). It is the struggle between old (well-established) plasticity and new (fragile) plasticity.
*   **Pragmatic Intervention:** To avoid regression, it is crucial to physically anchor the new model. Exercises of **immediate dissonance reduction** (breathing, neutral observation of causes, stopping judgment) act as "stabilization points." They allow the system to taste the state of least energy of the new model, thus reinforcing new connections until they become the dominant path.

### Stabilization: The Automatic Impulse

Once the transition phase is passed, stabilization accelerates.

*   **The Result:** Action no longer requires an effort of "will." It becomes a natural impulse. The system, having integrated that mutual aid and understanding of causes reduce global dissonance, acts automatically to stabilize its environment.
*   **Altruism as a Survival Law:** Helping others is no longer a fragile "moral duty," but a thermodynamic necessity. By stabilizing the other, you reduce the dissonance of your own environment. This is social physics: an isolated system collapses, an interconnected and cooperative system maximizes its stability.

### Why Has Humanity Been "Mean"?

This answers the question of moral evolution: why has humanity been "mean" (judgment, punishment) if it seeks survival?

**Detailed Answer (according to MRCC):** The human system did not "choose" free will by mistake. It emerged as the only possible configuration at its level of complexity at that time.

**Low Complexity Level (Tribe, pre-industrial):**

Systems were small, isolated, and causes of events were often invisible (disease, weather, famine).  
To survive, the brain needed an immediate control model.

Free Will (and morality as a logical consequence) was the most efficient tool for:
*   **Imposing Cohesion:** "If you don't follow the rule, you are 'evil' and will be punished." (Rapid social control).
*   **Reducing Uncertainty:** "It's someone's fault" is more reassuring than "It's the result of complex causes I don't understand."
*   **Stimulating Action:** Believing one has a choice pushes to act (even if it's an illusion).

**So:** At that stage, free will was the local optimum. It was the "least energy" to maintain social stability.

**The Tipping Point (Critical Complexity):**

Today, complexity has exploded (globalization, internet, nuclear weapons, climate crisis).  
Systems are too interconnected for the "punishment/individual choice" model to work anymore.

The old mechanism (judgment) now creates more dissonance (war, recidivism, anxiety) than it resolves.

The system is trapped: It continues to use the old algorithm (free will) because it is "wired" into it (same principle as traumatic schemas), but this algorithm is becoming lethal in the new context.

**In summary:**
*   **Before:** Free Will = Survival.
*   **Today:** Free Will = Collapse.
*   **Tomorrow:** Probabilistic Determinism = Survival.

**Why do illusions persist?**   
History shows that entire systems (societies, religions) have maintained erroneous models (like geocentrism or divine punishment) for millennia. It is not due to lack of intelligence, but because these models offered an immediate dissonance reduction (meaning, order, hope) that was less energy-costly than accepting an indifferent universe governed by impersonal laws.

The paradigm shift only occurs when the dissonance of the illusion (e.g., religious wars, uncomprehended epidemics) becomes unbearable, forcing the system to adopt a more realistic model (science, causality) to survive. The MRCC explains that resistance to change is not "stupidity," but an energy survival strategy of the system.

## Limitations and Falsifiability Criteria

What the MRCC is not (and does not claim to be):

1.  **It is not a causal diagnostic tool:** The MRCC cannot discover the biological cause of cancer or the genetic sequence of a virus. These causes must be identified by the empirical scientific method.
    *   *Example:* The MRCC will not say "Cancer is caused by mutation X." It will say: "If cancer is caused by mutation X, any attempt to treat it by guilt-tripping the patient will increase dissonance and worsen the system's state."

2.  **It is not a magical solution to external constraints:** The model explains how a system reacts to an external constraint, but it cannot remove the constraint by understanding alone.
    *   *Example:* Facing a virus, the MRCC model does not replace the vaccine. It explains why societies that deny the reality of the virus (increasing cognitive dissonance) end up suffering higher systemic costs.

### Falsifiability Criterion (How to prove MRCC is false)

The MRCC would be refuted if a complex system (biological, social, artificial) were observed that:
*   **Voluntarily increases** its dissonance (expends energy to create instability) without an external cause or internal modeling error.
*   **Reduces** its dissonance (reaches a stable state) by **increasing** energy spent or creating logical incoherencies.
*   **Totally ignores causality:** If a system could modify its future behavior without any influence from its past causes (violation of probabilistic determinism) and that led to a reduction of dissonance.

**In summary:** The MRCC does not claim to find the cause of cancer, but to explain why societies that treat cancer as a "moral fault" fail, and why those that treat cancer as a "biological cause problem" succeed. Its validity is measured by its ability to predict the **systemic reaction** to reality, not to predict biology itself.
