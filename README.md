The **MRCC** (Modèle de la Réaction Causale Complexée) is a unifying theoretical framework that redefines **consciousness, society, and complex system dynamics** through the lens of **thermodynamics and information theory**.

While the MRCC does not claim to replace General Relativity, it proposes a **structural isomorphism**: the "gravitational" pull of unresolved trauma or rigid beliefs in complex systems **mathematically mirrors** the curvature of spacetime caused by mass. In both regimes, high density leads to a reduction of possible states and an increase in deterministic behavior.

### Core Concepts
The MRCC postulates that while fundamental physics (gravity, quantum mechanics) governs the inert universe, **complex adaptive systems** (neurons, brains, societies, AI) operate under a specific emergent imperative: **the minimization of informational dissonance**.

*   **Consciousness:** Not a mystical entity, but the physical sensation of a system detecting the gap (dissonance) between its internal model and reality.
*   **Free Will:** An emergent illusion that creates systemic inefficiency (pain, conflict, guilt). True agency arises from understanding causality, not from "choosing" outside of it.
*   **Causality:** The only mechanism for true stability. By shifting from judging "choices" to understanding "causes," systems transition from turbulence to fluidity.

This model bridges the gap between **physics, psychology, and social dynamics**, offering a path from **magical hope** to **rational, systemic regulation**.

> **Scope Clarification:** Unlike previous hypotheses, the MRCC does not claim to replace General Relativity. Instead, it proposes that **dissonance acts as a "gravitational-like" force within the state space of complex systems**, while fundamental gravity remains the geometric constraint of spacetime.

*   **For Physicists & Mathematicians:** A hypothesis for **emergent dynamics** in complex systems. It proposes that "Dark Matter" in social/cognitive networks is the accumulated inertia of unresolved dissonance (trauma, rigid beliefs), offering a testable model for systemic stability without violating fundamental physics.
*   **For Psychologists & Sociologists:** A deterministic model explaining trauma, addiction, and social collapse as **"saturation of dissonance"** rather than moral failure or free-will errors. It provides a framework for intervention based on **causal restructuring** rather than punishment.
*   **For AI & Systems Engineers:** A guide to building robust, self-regulating agents that minimize friction (dissonance) rather than optimizing for arbitrary goals, leading to more stable and aligned AI behaviors.
*   **For Everyone:** A practical guide to reducing internal conflict by aligning with the laws of causality.
